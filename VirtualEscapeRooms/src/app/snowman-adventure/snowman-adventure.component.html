Snowman Adventure

I suppose it all started with the snow. You see, it's a very special kind of snow. A snow to make the happy happier and the giddy even giddier. A snow to make a homecoming homier and natural enemies friends, naturally. For it was the first snow of the season. And as any child can tell you, there's a certain magic to the very first snow, especially when it falls on the day before Christmas. For when the first snow is also a Christmas snow, well, something wonderful is bound to happen!

Let's build a snowman!

That's right, he's missing a hat! You search for a hat for the snowman and find several hats. One of these hats is a magic hat, but no one remembers which one it is. Everyone remembers a different detail about the magic hat.

When the magic hat is placed on his head, the snowman comes alive and starts to talk. "Happy birthday! Hey, I said my first words... But snowmen can't talk. Alright, come on now, what's the joke? Could, could I really be alive? I mean, I can make words, I can move. I can juggle, I can sweep, I can count to ten. One, two, three, four, five, nine, six, eight... Well, I can count to five. What do you know? I'm even ticklish. In fact, I'm all living. I am alive! What a neat thing to happen to a nice guy like me."

You and your friends decide to play with your new friend, the snowman. You go sledding together. You make snow angels. You have a snowball fight. After a while, the snowman looks worried. "I'm feeling hot. Is there a thermometer around here?"

Is it hot or cold?

What does it mean when the thermometer is mostly red?

The snowman is starting to sweat and sounds nervous. "Oh, I was afraid of that. When the thermometer gets all reddish, the temperature goes up. And when the temperature goes up, I start to melt. And when I start to melt... I get all wishy washy! The only place I'd never melt is the North Pole."

You decide to take the snowman to the train station so he can go to the North Pole. On the way there, you realize that you forgot to name the snowman. Find a way through town to the train station and figure out the snowman's name.

You bring Frosty to the train station. When you arrive, you realize that none of you brought any money. You'll have to be sneaky to get on the train. Frosty and you slip into a refrigerated boxcar heading north. It's full of ice cream and frozen Christmas cakes. Now, a refrigerated boxcar is a splendid way to travel. Splendid that is... if one is a snowman. People, on the other hand, get dangerously cold. So when the little freight train stopped to let an express train full of happy Christmas travelers pass, everyone took advantage of teh opportunity and quickly got off.

You come upon a tiny glen which seems almost magical. For it's Christmas Eve, and the woodland animals are all decorating for their big celebration. They know Santa is to come that night, and they wanted everything to be jsut right. The animals are delighted to help all of you get warm. The animals bring lots of sticks to a spot away from the glen so the fire wouldn't spread to the trees.

Soon there was a spark, and in no time a splendid fire is crackling away. While the fire is being lit, the sun goes down, and it gets even colder. The fire helps, but it isn't enough to keep everyone warm in the cold winter wind. Fortunately, the light from the fire reflects off windows from further in the trees.

Right at the bottom of the hill was a tiny greenhouse used to grow precious tropical poinsettias for Christmas. Frosty says, "It's got to be all warm and snug inside for those Christmas flowers to grow so beautiful. Let's go in. I'll only stay inside for a minute. Besides, I've been meaning to take off a little weight anyway."

Once everyone is inside, the wind blows the door shut. You try to open it, but it's locked. Oh no! We need someone to help us get out of the greenhouse before Frosty melts, and we still need to get him to the North Pole.

You search through the greenhouse and find a phone, but someone cut up the phone book.

You reach Santa Claus on the phone. He promises to help, but he is in the middle of delivering presents and won't be there until morning. Meanwhile, Frosty is melting fast. You'll have to figure out how to open the greenhouse door yourself. Maybe you can find some clues by looking around.

Santa arrives to a terrible sight. You were just a little too slow unlocking the greenhouse, and Frosty completely melted. Santa is undeterred. "Too late? Why, nonsense!", he says. "He's not gone for good. You see, he was made out of Christmas snow, and Christmas snow can never disappear completely. Oh, it sometimes goes away for almost a year at a time and takes the form of spring and summer rain, but you can bet your boots that when a good jolly December wind kisses it, it will turn into Christmas snow all over again!"

Santa holds the greenhouse door open for the wind to blow in. Frosty reforms and the wind lifts the magic hat up onto his head, bringing him back to life.

Santa takes you home and prepares to bring Frosty back to the North Pole. As Santa promised, Frosty returned every year with the magical Christmas snow. And every year, there was a great celebration with a great Christmas parade. And with Frosty the Snowman, Christmas was always very merry indeed. You have a merry Christmas, too!
